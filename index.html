Abstract

In this work, we conduct a comprehensive evaluation of parameter-efficient fine-tuning (PEFT) methods applied to pre-trained speech models. Although large-scale pre-trained models have proven useful in various downstream speech applications, the efficiency of each PEFT individual module for specific speech tasks remains under-explored. We therefore put forth a series of experiments to investigate different scales and configurations of parameter-efficient modules and document their impact on different target speech tasks under cross-task settings. Specifically, we evaluate the PEFT modules on (i) the SUPERB benchmark for different speech tasks, and (ii) the ML-SUPERB benchmarks for low-resource language settings. Furthermore, we have deviced a novel metric, referred to Adapter Performance Metric (APM), that takes into account the performance gain when assessing a fine-tuning method. Our empirical results provide a valuable guidance for future developments in speech applications.
